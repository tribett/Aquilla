# Aquilla — Full Script

Christian fantasy adventure across five chapters and eight dungeons. Mercy restores; grace is received, not earned.

---

## Chapter I — Briarfold Valley

### Opening — Elder Mara

**Elder Mara:** Aquilla, the Fold lies tangled and the sheep are scattered. Briarfold has grown afraid of its own calling.

**Elder Mara:** Take Bracken and the Shepherd's Staff. Gather what is lost, guard what is weak, and restore what mercy can heal.

**Aquilla:** I am afraid, Elder Mara. What if I am too small for this?

**Elder Mara:** Courage is obedience while fear is still speaking. Go gently. Grace goes ahead of you.

### Briarfold — Elder Mara (repeat visits)

**Elder Mara:** Mercy is not weakness, Aquilla. It is strength that bends low enough to lift the lost.

**Elder Mara:** The Fold was built for refuge, not pride. Restore it, and Briarfold will remember its calling.

**Elder Mara:** Courage is obedience while fear is still speaking. Take the staff and go gently.

### Dungeon I — The Fold of the Lost

- Gather three lost sheep with Bracken.
- Restore the dry spring with the Shepherd's Staff.
- Calm the corrupted guardian: distract with Bracken, steady with the staff.
- Ring the fold-bell; enter the Fold dungeon.
- Restore the Fold's heart shrine.

**Guardian (calmed):** The guardian lowers its head and remembers its charge.

**Fold restored:** The Fold breathes again. Briarfold remembers refuge, not fear.

### Old Pasture

**Fear echo (calmed):** The fear echo releases its borrowed voice and becomes still.

**Hidden grove:** Aquilla receives the Grove Lantern as gift, not prize.

### Dungeon II — Lantern Ruins (Beacon Hall)

Light creed beacons in order:

1. **Maker:** The Maker beacon answers: the Father made all things good and calls them beloved.
2. **Redeemer:** The Redeemer beacon answers: the Son seeks the lost by mercy, not by force.
3. **Giver:** The Giver beacon answers: the Spirit makes dry bones live; the Lantern Ruins shine with received grace.

### Dungeon III — Sanctum of Witness

1. **Remember:** Aquilla remembers: the Light came first, and every mercy began as gift.
2. **Receive:** Aquilla receives: grace is not earned by courage; it is given in Christ.
3. **Return:** Aquilla returns: the Shepherd has sent restored servants back to love the valley.

**Sanctum complete:** Aquilla walks home through the green road, sent in mercy rather than pride.

### Chapter I Finale — Elder Mara

**Elder Mara:** You returned, Aquilla. Not with trophies, but with the lost gathered and the Fold made refuge again.

**Elder Mara:** You remembered that light is gift. You received that grace is not earned. You return now as one sent.

**Aquilla:** The valley is wider than I knew. I thought courage meant not being afraid.

**Elder Mara:** Courage means faithful care while fear still speaks. The Shepherd sends restored servants to love what remains broken.

**Elder Mara:** Rest tonight in Briarfold. Tomorrow the road opens toward Elarion's wider kingdom. Gather. Guard. Restore.

**Aquilla:** I will go where mercy calls. Bracken and I are ready.

---

## Chapter II — Ashen Moor

### Ashford Crossing — Pilgrim Elias

**Pilgrim Elias:** The Ashen Moor burns with borrowed light. False fire pretends to guide travelers who have lost the Shepherd's voice.

**Pilgrim Elias:** Cleanse Ember Fen first. Its channels were turned to feed pride. Turn them back toward mercy.

**Aquilla:** I will not strike what can be restored. Bracken and I go gently.

### Dungeon IV — Ember Fen Cistern

**Ember Fen Sentinel (calmed):** The fen sentinel bows; false fire yields to living water.

Redirect channels north → center → south. Cleanse the heart shrine.

**Ember Fen cleansed:** Aquilla redirects the fen channels; false fire dies and true light returns.

### Dungeon V — Ashen Spire

**False-Light Archon (calmed):** The False-Light Archon scatters; received light remains.

**Lantern claimed:** Aquilla receives the Lantern of Witness: received light that reveals what is hidden.

---

## Chapter III — High Kingsroad

### Kingsroad Undercroft

Clear three thorn snares. Open the undercroft seal.

**Waymark:** The waymark reads: 'Walk mercy; the King's servants remember.'

### Dungeon VI — Monastic Vault

**Memory Thief (calmed):** Stolen hymns return to the vault; memory is mercy's treasure again.

**Monastic Echo:** We sang when the road was whole. Sing faith before sight returns.

Sing hymns in order — faith, hope, love:

- **Faith:** A monk's voice returns: 'We believed before we saw the King's mercy.'
- **Hope:** Stone echoes hope: 'The Shepherd still walks the ruined roads.'
- **Love:** Love answers last: 'Mercy is the monastery's true foundation.'

**Harp melody:** Play keys 1–5 in order at the choir altar. Claim the Harp of Remembrance.

---

## Chapter IV — Elarion

### Elarion Gate

**Inscription:** False light cannot crown what grace has redeemed.

### Forgotten Cathedral — Choir

**Cathedral Acolyte:** Praise remembers who made all things good. Confession remembers we did not keep that goodness alone.

**Cathedral Acolyte:** Sending is not escape—it is being sent back to love what remains broken.

Worship in order: praise → confession → sending.

### Dungeon VIII — Lucent Depths

**Lucent Court Sentinel:** We offer unity without cost. Bow, and fear will leave you.

**Aquilla:** False light binds. Mercy restores without demanding pride.

**Lucent Court (calmed):** False unity shatters. The Lucent Court scatters before received light.

**Throne waymark:** Aquilla walks the green road home for the Crown Witness.

---

## Chapter V — Crown Witness

### Elder Mara — Crown Witness

**Elder Mara:** You crossed moor, kingsroad, and Elarion's broken courts. False light could not hold you.

**Elder Mara:** The Crown Witness is not a trophy. It is faithfulness: gather, guard, restore, wherever mercy calls.

**Aquilla:** The kingdom is wider than fear told me. I am sent to love what remains broken.

**Elder Mara:** Then rest, and rise. Aquilla's witness is complete. The Shepherd's mercy goes with you.

## Theme

Mercy seeks the lost. Grace is gift. Courage is obedience while fear still speaks. Aquilla is sent—not as one who saved himself, but as one who was restored.
