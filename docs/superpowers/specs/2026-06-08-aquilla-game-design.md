# Aquilla Game Design

Date: 2026-06-08
Status: Approved concept design, pending implementation plan

## Summary

Aquilla is a top-down 2D Christian fantasy adventure inspired by early Zelda, A Link Between Worlds, Breath of the Wild, Ocarina of Time, Fable, Dragon Age, C.S. Lewis, and J.R.R. Tolkien. It follows Aquilla, a shepherd boy, as he grows from fear into faithful sonship by gathering, guarding, and restoring what has been scattered.

The game uses an original allegorical fantasy world with biblical-history adjacent foundations. It should feel creative, intuitive, and fun, while remaining biblically sound and within historic Christian orthodoxy.

The first playable slice is a Zelda-like sacred adventure: a small green valley, one village, one shrine-dungeon, staff-based puzzles, a border-collie-styled sheepdog companion, non-lethal conflict, and a restoration-focused ending.

## Core Vision

Aquilla is a top-down 2D sacred adventure about a shepherd boy called into courage, humility, and faithful sonship. The first slice plays like early Zelda and A Link Between Worlds: a small overworld, secrets, item-gated traversal, a companion, and one compact shrine-dungeon.

The world is original fantasy, but its structure is biblical: exile and restoration, shepherd and flock, lost memory, ruined worship, false guidance, mercy over domination, and courage formed through obedience. Aquilla's first heroic act is not conquest; it is gathering and guarding what has been scattered.

The opening region is a bright ancient green kingdom marked by exile-frontier scars. It includes remnant villages, ancient wells, ruined holy places, secret groves, overgrown paths, and scattered signs of a forgotten calling.

The theological guardrail is broad Nicene orthodoxy, with evangelical clarity and ancient Christian texture. Grace is not treated as a magic system, salvation is not earned by collectibles, and divine realities are handled through reverent allegory rather than direct trivialization.

## Influences

Gameplay references:

- Early Zelda: clear top-down movement, compact secrets, readable dungeons, item-gated traversal.
- A Link Between Worlds: dense overworld, flexible exploration, satisfying dungeon readability.
- Ocarina of Time and Breath of the Wild: wonder, discovery, ancient mystery, courage under calling.
- Fable and Dragon Age: moral stakes, village texture, character growth, consequence-rich storytelling.

Literary references:

- C.S. Lewis: allegorical moral clarity, childlike wonder, temptation through distorted desire, courage and humility.
- J.R.R. Tolkien: sub-created world depth, providence, exile, restoration, evil as corruption of the good, hope under sorrow.

These influences are inspiration only. Aquilla should be its own world, not a direct adaptation or imitation.

## Theology And Orthodoxy

Aquilla should be Christian in its moral and metaphysical structure, not merely decorated with religious language. The story assumes:

- Creation is good.
- Evil is a corruption of good, not an equal opposite to good.
- Truth is received rather than invented.
- Humility is strength.
- Repentance opens the path to restoration.
- Sacrificial love is greater than domination.
- Mercy and justice are both real.
- Hope is grounded in providence, not optimism alone.

Guardrails:

- God is not reduced to a collectible, power meter, spell source, or ordinary NPC.
- Grace is gift, not earned progression.
- Prayer and worship themes are reverent and not treated like spellcasting.
- Evil can deceive through beauty, safety, order, and false wisdom, not only ugliness.
- Aquilla's growth is formation, not self-divinization.
- Scripture can inspire motifs, but the game must not rewrite biblical events as though Aquilla is a biblical hero.
- The world can include church-like, liturgical, and monastic textures, but the first slice should avoid unnecessary denominational specificity.

Tone:

- Wondrous, serious, hopeful, and adventurous.
- Fun, mysterious, and occasionally frightening.
- Not cynical, grimdark, preachy, or shallowly moralistic.

The player should feel the theology through verbs: gather, guard, restore, discern, repent, forgive, and enter danger for the sake of another.

## World

The first region is part of a once-faithful kingdom called Elarion. It is green and ancient, but marked by exile-frontier wounds. Its people still live in the land, yet spiritually and culturally they live like exiles.

Opening village:

- Name: Briarfold.
- Role: Aquilla's home village.
- Identity: a remnant village near old pasturelands and a ruined fold sanctuary.
- Spiritual condition: fearful, practical, forgetful, and resigned.

First dungeon:

- Name: The Fold of the Lost.
- Identity: an overgrown sheepfold sanctuary.
- Function: a refuge that once gathered and protected the vulnerable.
- Current state: tangled by thorn, fear, neglect, and a false call that scatters rather than gathers.

Antagonistic presence:

- Name: the Lucent Court.
- Nature: an empire or cult of false light.
- Temptation: safety without repentance, wisdom without humility, unity without truth.
- First-slice presence: symbols, a strange beacon, environmental distortion, and one corrupted guardian.

## Main Character

Aquilla is a boy from Briarfold. He is a shepherd before he is a hero.

Starting traits:

- Fearful but tender-hearted.
- Attentive to the vulnerable.
- More faithful in small duties than he realizes.
- Unsure that he is brave or important.

Core arc:

- Begins in fear.
- Learns courage as obedience under fear.
- Grows into sonship through faithful care.
- Chooses protection and restoration over self-protection.

Aquilla's heroic identity should feel rooted in shepherding: patience, attention, protection, guidance, and willingness to go after the lost.

## Sheepdog Companion

Aquilla has a sheepdog sidekick styled after a border collie.

The dog should be a real companion, not just a tool. It has three roles:

1. Puzzle partner.
   - Fetches small objects.
   - Tracks scent trails.
   - Herds sheep and timid creatures.
   - Holds pressure plates.
   - Reaches small spaces Aquilla cannot.

2. Protection companion.
   - Warns of danger.
   - Distracts threats.
   - Interrupts attacks.
   - Guards vulnerable NPCs.
   - Helps subdue corrupted creatures without killing them.

3. Emotional and story companion.
   - Reacts to danger, sheep, holy places, and corruption.
   - Shows Aquilla's tenderness and fear through animation.
   - Encourages the player to care rather than dominate.
   - Creates warmth and attachment across the adventure.

The dog should feel intelligent and loyal, with readable behavior and simple commands.

## First Signature Item

The first signature item is the Shepherd's Staff.

Core uses:

- Move certain stones and light obstacles.
- Redirect simple water channels.
- Ring old fold-bells.
- Open shepherd gates.
- Stun or calm corrupted creatures.
- Guide sheep through narrow paths.
- Interact with old sanctuary mechanisms.

The staff should communicate humble authority. It is not a power fantasy weapon first; it is an instrument of guidance, protection, and restoration.

Later major tools can include:

- Lantern of Witness: reveals hidden paths, dispels illusions, exposes false light.
- Harp of Remembrance: restores broken shrines, opens memory echoes, calms creatures, awakens old covenant memory.

## Gameplay

The first playable slice should prove exploration, puzzle solving, companion use, and non-lethal action.

Player movement:

- Top-down 2D movement.
- Readable collision.
- Interact button for signs, NPCs, gates, bells, objects, and dungeon fixtures.
- Staff action button.
- Dog command button or radial command menu.

Exploration:

- Small overworld connecting Briarfold, old pasture, hidden grove, dry channel, and The Fold of the Lost.
- Secrets reward observation and care.
- Item-gated traversal uses the Shepherd's Staff and dog commands.

Puzzles:

- Shepherd gates that open when sheep are gathered.
- Bells that call or calm creatures.
- Stones and bramble barriers moved or cleared by the staff.
- Water channels redirected to revive dry ground.
- Pressure plates held by Aquilla, the dog, or sheep.
- Scent trails followed by the dog.

Conflict:

- Mostly non-lethal.
- Enemies can be avoided, stunned, calmed, freed from snares, or restored.
- Some danger remains necessary for readable stakes.
- The primary fantasy is shepherding, rescue, and restoration, not clearing rooms through violence.

Enemy examples:

- Thorn-tangled beasts.
- Corrupted fold guardians.
- Shadowed wolves.
- Fear echoes that scatter sheep.
- Environmental hazards such as brambles, collapsing stones, and blocked water.

## First Dungeon: The Fold Of The Lost

The Fold of the Lost is the first compact shrine-dungeon.

Purpose:

- Tie together Aquilla's shepherd background, the sheepdog companion, non-lethal conflict, and theology.
- Teach that courage means faithful care.
- Let the player restore a refuge rather than conquer a lair.

Dungeon loop:

1. Enter the overgrown outer fold.
2. Find scattered sheep and learn basic dog herding.
3. Use the staff to open shepherd gates and ring old fold-bells.
4. Redirect water to revive dry ground and expose a hidden path.
5. Calm or subdue corrupted guardians without killing them.
6. Gather lost sheep into the inner fold.
7. Face the corrupted guardian.
8. Restore the fold and return to Briarfold.

The guardian should not be evil by nature. It should be wounded, bound, deceived, or corrupted. The resolution should show restoration, not annihilation.

## First Playable Slice Scope

The first build is a vertical slice, not the full game.

Included:

- One opening village: Briarfold.
- One small overworld: green valley, old pasture, ruined paths, hidden grove, dry channel, locked shepherd gates.
- One dungeon: The Fold of the Lost.
- One signature tool: Shepherd's Staff.
- One companion: border-collie-styled sheepdog.
- One main objective: gather scattered sheep, restore the fold, calm the corrupted guardian.
- One ending beat: Aquilla returns with the lost gathered and receives the next call toward the wider kingdom.

Core systems to prove:

- Top-down movement and collision.
- Interactables: gates, stones, bells, water channels, pressure plates.
- Staff actions.
- Dog commands: follow, stay, fetch, herd, distract.
- Simple non-lethal enemy behavior.
- Dialogue and sign text.
- Save or reset of slice progress.
- Pixel-art visual style with readable Zelda-like proportions.

Out of scope for the first slice:

- Full kingdom map.
- Multiple towns.
- Full RPG party system.
- Skill trees.
- Large equipment inventory.
- Complex morality system.
- Branching campaign story.
- Denominational theology simulator.
- Online features.

## Technical Direction

The game should be built as a browser-playable 2D game first, so it can be tested quickly and shared easily.

Likely stack:

- TypeScript.
- A proven 2D game framework, preferably Phaser, unless project constraints later suggest another option.
- Vite or similar fast local dev tooling.
- Vitest for unit tests.
- Playwright for browser and canvas smoke checks.

Rationale:

- Phaser gives proven 2D scene, sprite, input, collision, camera, and animation primitives.
- TypeScript keeps game state and puzzle rules testable.
- Browser delivery supports fast iteration and visual verification.

The implementation should keep game rules testable outside the renderer:

- Game state.
- Puzzle completion rules.
- Dog command state.
- Inventory and tool unlocks.
- Dungeon objective progression.

Renderer code should consume these rules rather than hiding all behavior inside scene callbacks.

## Testing And Verification

The project should follow TDD for implementation.

Test-first areas:

- Game state transitions.
- Puzzle rules.
- Dog command state.
- Staff interactions.
- Inventory/tool unlocks.
- Dungeon completion.
- Non-lethal enemy resolution.

Browser verification:

- Game loads without console errors.
- Canvas renders nonblank content.
- Movement input changes player position.
- UI and text fit on desktop and mobile viewport sizes.
- The dog appears and follows or obeys commands.
- The first dungeon can be completed in a scripted smoke path.

Manual feel pass:

- Movement feels responsive.
- The dog feels like a companion, not a cursor.
- The first puzzles are readable without tutorial bloat.
- Non-lethal conflict still feels engaging.
- The theology is present through play, not only exposition.

## Open Design Decisions For Implementation Planning

These are intentionally deferred to the implementation plan:

- Exact engine setup commands.
- Pixel art asset workflow.
- Final dog name.
- Exact control scheme.
- Save format.
- Dialogue format.
- Map editor or tilemap approach.
- First-slice milestone breakdown.

These are planning details, not unresolved design contradictions.
